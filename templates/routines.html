Form Tips

Proper Posture

  • Stand tall with shoulders back and down
  • Engage your core throughout the movement
  • Pull with your arms — don't let them drag
  • Full range of motion on every stride
  • Keep your gaze forward, not down

Grip Variations

  • Standard: palms facing the handles, overhand grip
  • Reverse: palms facing you, underhand grip — hits biceps harder
  • Alternating: switch grip each round for balanced development

Stride Variations

  • Long stride: deeper knee drive, more glute activation
  • Short stride: higher cadence, cardio emphasis
  • Climbing squats: add a squat motion on each step
  • Sprints: maximum effort, shortest recovery

Warm-up & Cool-down

  • Always warm up 3–5 minutes before going hard
  • Start with easy pacing, gradually increase intensity
  • Cool down 2–3 minutes after finishing
  • Stretch calves, quads, hamstrings, and shoulders
